\begin{abstract}
We study Liouville-type nonexistence properties for a class of infinity elliptic equations involving gradient-dependent nonlinearities and Hardy--Hénon weights. 
Working within the viscosity solution framework, we identify critical exponents associated with the intrinsic cubic structure of the infinity Laplacian and establish conditions under which nontrivial entire solutions cannot exist.

We further analyze the interaction between diffusion, gradient effects, and spatial weights, revealing threshold phenomena in both reaction and gradient growth terms. 
These results contribute to the qualitative theory of highly degenerate elliptic equations and suggest connections to Lipschitz regularization in modern machine learning.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Liouville-type theorems play a central role in nonlinear elliptic theory, providing rigidity results that preclude the existence of nontrivial entire solutions under suitable structural conditions \cite{gidas1981}. 

The classical Lane--Emden equation
\[
-\Delta u = u^p \quad \text{in } \mathbb{R}^n
\]
has been extensively studied, with sharp nonexistence thresholds depending on the dimension. Extensions to weighted models, such as the Hardy--Hénon equation, further reveal the influence of spatial heterogeneity on critical exponents \cite{le2025}.

In contrast, the infinity Laplacian
\[
\Delta_\infty u = \langle D^2 u \nabla u, \nabla u \rangle
\]
introduces strong degeneracy and requires analysis in the viscosity sense \cite{crandall1992}. This operator arises naturally in the theory of absolutely minimizing Lipschitz extensions \cite{aronsson1967}.

In this work, we study equations of the form
\[
-\Delta_\infty u + c|\nabla u|^q = \lambda |x|^a u^p \quad \text{in } \mathbb{R}^n,
\]
focusing on Liouville-type nonexistence results under combined gradient and weighted nonlinear effects.

\section{Preliminaries}

We work within the framework of viscosity solutions as developed by Crandall, Ishii, and Lions \cite{crandall1992}. 

\begin{definition}
A continuous function $u$ is a viscosity solution if it satisfies the differential inequality in the sense of test functions.
\end{definition}

\section{Main Results}

\begin{theorem}
Let $u$ be a non-negative viscosity solution of
\[
-\Delta_\infty u = \lambda |x|^a u^p.
\]
If $1 < p \le 3$, then $u \equiv 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}
Let $u$ satisfy
\[
-\Delta_\infty u + c|\nabla u|^q \ge \lambda |x|^a u^p.
\]
If $q > 3$ and $p < q$, then $u \equiv 0$ under suitable decay conditions.
\end{theorem}

\section{Connections to Machine Learning}

The infinity Laplacian is closely related to Lipschitz regularization and has connections to robust optimization and adversarial learning \cite{finlay2018, gao2022}. 

These connections suggest that gradient constraints in PDEs may provide theoretical insight into stability in machine learning models.

\section{Conclusion}

We established Liouville-type nonexistence results for infinity elliptic equations with gradient and weighted nonlinearities, identifying critical thresholds that govern solution behavior.

Future work includes extensions to parabolic models and more general nonlinear structures.

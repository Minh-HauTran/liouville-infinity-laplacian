# Liouville-type Results for Infinity Elliptic Equations with Gradient and Hardy–Hénon Nonlinearities

## 📌 Overview

This repository contains research conducted at the **Vietnam Institute for Advanced Study in Mathematics (VIASM)** during the Summer 2025 Research Fellowship (Aug–Nov 2025).

We study qualitative properties of solutions to a class of highly nonlinear elliptic equations of infinity-Laplacian type, focusing on **Liouville-type theorems** in the presence of:

* Gradient-dependent nonlinearities
* Hardy–Hénon-type weighted nonlinear structures

Our results contribute to the broader program of understanding rigidity, nonexistence, and qualitative behavior of solutions to nonlinear PDEs beyond classical uniformly elliptic settings.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement

Liouville-type theorems play a central role in nonlinear analysis, providing fundamental insights into the **nonexistence of nontrivial global solutions** under structural constraints.

In this project, we investigate:

* Whether entire (global) solutions exist for a class of infinity elliptic equations
* How **gradient dependence** alters classical Liouville phenomena
* The impact of **Hardy–Hénon weights** on qualitative solution behavior

These questions remain largely open in nonstandard elliptic frameworks and require refined analytical techniques.

---

## 🧠 Main Contributions

* Established new **Liouville-type nonexistence results** for infinity elliptic equations
* Incorporated **gradient-dependent nonlinearities** into the analytical framework
* Extended Liouville theory to **Hardy–Hénon-type weighted settings**
* Developed rigorous arguments combining:

  * Comparison principles
  * Scaling and blow-up techniques
  * A priori estimates
* Provided new insights into the interplay between degeneracy, nonlinearity, and spatial weights

---

## 🧾 Abstract

We investigate Liouville-type properties for a class of infinity elliptic equations with nonlinearities depending on both the gradient and Hardy–Hénon-type spatial weights. By combining techniques from nonlinear elliptic theory, scaling analysis, and a priori estimates, we establish nonexistence results under suitable structural assumptions. Our findings extend classical Liouville theorems to a highly degenerate and nonuniform setting, shedding light on the qualitative structure of solutions to infinity-Laplacian-type equations.

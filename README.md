# Liouville-Type Results for Infinity Elliptic Equations with Gradient and Hardy–Hénon Nonlinearities

## 📌 Overview

This repository contains research conducted at the Vietnam Institute for Advanced Study in Mathematics (VIASM) during the Summer 2025 Research Fellowship (Aug–Nov 2025).

We study qualitative properties of solutions to a class of highly nonlinear elliptic equations of infinity-Laplacian type, with a focus on Liouville-type nonexistence phenomena in the presence of:

- Gradient-dependent nonlinearities  
- Hardy–Hénon-type weighted structures  

This work contributes to the broader study of rigidity, degeneracy, and qualitative behavior of solutions in non-uniformly elliptic settings.

---

## ⭐ Key Results (Quick View)

- Identification of a critical exponent associated with the cubic structure of the infinity Laplacian  
- Gradient domination threshold governing the influence of nonlinear first-order terms  
- Liouville-type nonexistence results under structural and scaling constraints  
- Extension of Liouville theory to weighted and gradient-dependent regimes  

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement

Liouville-type theorems play a fundamental role in nonlinear analysis by characterizing when global (entire) solutions must be trivial.

In this project, we investigate:

- Existence vs. nonexistence of entire solutions for infinity elliptic equations  
- The role of gradient dependence in modifying classical Liouville phenomena  
- The impact of Hardy–Hénon weights on solution behavior  

These questions remain largely open in degenerate elliptic frameworks and require refined analytical techniques beyond classical uniformly elliptic theory.

---

## 🧠 Main Contributions

We investigate and derive Liouville-type nonexistence results for infinity elliptic equations with combined nonlinear effects. Specifically, we:

- Incorporate gradient-dependent nonlinearities into the analytical framework  
- Extend Liouville-type analysis to Hardy–Hénon weighted structures  
- Develop arguments based on:
  - Comparison principles  
  - Scaling and blow-up techniques  
  - A priori estimates  

Our analysis highlights the interplay between degeneracy, nonlinearity, and spatial weights in determining qualitative solution behavior.

---

## 🧾 Abstract

We investigate Liouville-type properties for a class of infinity elliptic equations with nonlinearities depending on both the gradient and Hardy–Hénon-type spatial weights. By combining techniques from nonlinear elliptic theory, scaling analysis, and a priori estimates, we derive nonexistence results under suitable structural assumptions. The analysis reveals a critical exponent phenomenon linked to the intrinsic cubic structure of the infinity Laplacian, and provides insight into the qualitative structure of solutions in highly degenerate elliptic regimes.

## 🔗 Interdisciplinary Insight

The infinity Laplacian is closely related to absolutely minimizing Lipschitz extensions and arises in optimization and control problems.

From a modern perspective, this connects to:

- Lipschitz regularization in optimization  
- Gradient constraints in robust machine learning  
- Stability of models under adversarial perturbations  

These connections suggest that structural properties of nonlinear PDEs may inform theoretical aspects of robustness and generalization.
